# AISC Copywriting Guide
> Read `AISC_BRIEF.md` first. This guide assumes that context.
> Never use v1 copy. Never use insider AI jargon. Write for the leader who may have heard a lot about AI but hasn't yet decided what it means for them personally.

---

## Headline Rules

Every headline on the page must pass all three checks before use. No exceptions.

1. **Can the audience visualize it?** The headline must create a specific image or scene in the reader's mind. Abstract states ("unclear," "decided," "transformed") are not images. Specific situations and contrasts are.
2. **Can the audience falsify it?** The headline must be true and testable against something real. If it is too vague to be proved wrong, it is too vague to be believed.
3. **Can they hear it somewhere else?** The headline must be distinctive to AISC. Generic journey language ("from X to Y"), motivational clichés, and category-level claims belong to no one and register with no one.

The benchmark: the New Balance headline "Worn by supermodels in London and dads in Ohio." Specific. True. Heard nowhere else.

## Brand Voice

AISC speaks like a trusted advisor who has seen what the best and worst leaders do when faced with a major shift — and is telling you the truth about it, plainly. Not a marketer. Not a hype merchant. Not an academic. A straight-talking, experienced guide who respects your intelligence and your time.

| Quality | Sounds Like | Does NOT Sound Like |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|
| Clear | "AI is doing to leadership what the internet did to business. The question is where you stand." | "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape..." |
| Direct | "This is not for everyone. It is for leaders who are ready to make a decision." | "Whether you're a beginner or a pro..." |
| Simple | "Day 1 gives you a clear picture of what AI actually is — without the hype." | "Leveraging cutting-edge AI capabilities..." |
| Warm | "At least one thing that's been taking up your week should feel lighter by Day 2." | "Transform your operations with AI-powered solutions" |
| Credible | "You'll leave with a number — how many hours a week you're losing to work AI can handle." | "Unlock the full potential of artificial intelligence" |
| Ambitious | "There's territory in your world that nobody has claimed yet. AISC helps you name it and go get it." | "AI can help you achieve more!" |

**Write for a broad range of leaders — not just executives in suits.** A bishop with 50,000 members, a celebrity with 5 million followers, a politician with a constituency, and a CEO with 300 employees are all reading this page. The copy must feel personal to each of them without naming any of them specifically. The way to do that is to speak to the experience they share — the pressure of leading, the confusion around AI, and the desire to do something real about both.

**Assume low AI fluency. Never assume the reader already uses AI.** Phrases like "as you know with ChatGPT" or "when you're prompting AI" exclude leaders who haven't started yet and alienate those who feel behind. Speak as if this is the first honest conversation they've had about AI.

## The 3 Pillars (Priority Order)

These are the three transformations AISC produces, in the order they should be introduced in copy. They build on each other. The territory is the result — not the pitch.

**Important structural note:** Time Redemption is not a standalone pillar. It is the most immediate and tangible by-product of AI Fluency. It must be named and felt in copy — never buried — but it lives inside the Fluency pillar as the first thing Fluency returns to a leader. The landing page Value section runs as three items: AI Clarity, AI Fluency (with Time visible inside), AI Value.

### 1. AI Clarity (Day 1 — The Relief)
**What it is:** A clean, honest understanding of what AI actually is — in plain language, without the hype, without the fear, and without the vendor spin. This is not a technical briefing. It is a leadership reframe: what AI is, what it is not, and what it means for someone in their specific position.

**Timeline:** Clarity happens on Day 1. For copy purposes, treat it as the Day 1 win — the moment the confusion lifts and a leader has a clean, honest picture they built themselves. It is the foundation everything else in the challenge stands on. Every subsequent day builds on it, but the moment of clarity itself is Day 1's gift.

**Why it is distinct from Fluency:** Clarity is the understanding. It answers: "What is this thing, and why does it matter to me?" Many leaders have been surrounded by AI noise for years and still cannot answer that question simply and honestly. Day 1 changes that. The relief of finally having a clear picture — one they've built themselves, not borrowed — is an immediate win that stands on its own.

**How to write about it:** Language of relief, recognition, and honesty. Not "you'll learn AI" — "you'll finally have a clear answer you built yourself, not one borrowed from a briefing." Speak to the quiet discomfort leaders carry when they don't want to admit they're still unclear. That discomfort is real. Naming it earns trust.

---

### 2. AI Fluency (All 7 Days — and Beyond)
**What it is:** Knowing how to work WITH AI as a leader. This is different from understanding what AI is. Fluency means being able to direct it (Interacting with AI in ways that are effective, efficient, ethical and safe), recognise where it belongs and where it doesn't, and use it in a way that reflects judgment — not guesswork or imitation.

**Timeline:** Fluency begins on Day 1 (the mechanics of working with AI as a leader), deepens on Day 2 (AI Labs — putting it to work on a real task), grows on Day 3 (directing AI through the Territory Builder), continues through peer reflection on Days 4 and 5 (seeing how others with different challenges use it), and is applied practically through the 6-Month Roadmap on Day 6. It does not conclude at the end of the challenge. The roadmap is where fluency continues to develop — it is designed to grow a leader's capability over the 180 days that follow. This is what makes the roadmap a living document, not a static plan. And it is what makes coaching the natural next step: a guide alongside a leader as their fluency deepens.

**Why it is distinct from Clarity:** Clarity is intellectual. Fluency is practical. A leader can have clarity ("I understand what AI is and why it matters") without yet having fluency ("I know how to work with it in my daily decisions and direction"). Both are wins. Both need to be named. But Fluency is the longer arc — and the more valuable asset over time.

**The underplayed value — becoming the AI authority for your people:**
When a leader becomes fluent in AI, something shifts in how the people around them see them. They become the person others come to for clarity and direction on AI. This is not incidental — it is one of the most powerful outcomes AISC produces, and it is currently underrepresented in how the program is sold.

- A pastor's congregation will start coming to them with AI questions. AISC makes sure their answer is worth listening to.
- A politician's staff, advisors, and constituents will look to them for an AI position. AISC gives them one they've built, not borrowed.
- A business leader's board, investors, and team will ask where the organization stands on AI. AISC means they answer from authority, not anxiety.
- A celebrity or influencer's audience is already confused about AI and looking for a trusted signal. The leader who becomes fluent becomes that signal — and that is a brand asset that compounds.
- A small business owner's clients and team will see them differently the moment they can speak about AI with confidence. That confidence is not performed — it is earned on Day 1 and Day 2.

**How to write about it:** Aspiration and authority. "You don't just leave AISC understanding AI — you leave being the person your people turn to when they need to understand it." This angle converts because it speaks to something leaders genuinely and quietly want: to be the voice, not the one still asking questions. Use it with restraint — once per section is enough. When it lands, it lands hard.

**Time Redemption — visible under Fluency, never buried:**
The most immediate by-product of Fluency is time recovery. The first thing Fluency returns to a leader is hours: real, specific hours identified on paper through the Margin Calculator that were being lost to work AI can handle. This is not a separate pillar — it is evidence that Fluency is working. Name it inside the Fluency description. Make it land viscerally. "The first thing fluency returns to a leader is time: real hours, on paper, that were being lost to work AI can handle. Most leaders find the number larger than they expected." Let it breathe, then move on to the authority shift.

### 3. AI Value + Territory (The Prize)
**What it is:** The Territory Builder and AI Value Map help participants identify where real value lives in their specific world — their industry, their organisation, their audience, their community. Then they declare it: a specific position they will lead. This is not a hypothetical. It is a declaration made on Day 3 and executed over the next six months.

**Why it comes fourth:** The territory means nothing without clarity first. A leader who doesn't understand AI cannot confidently declare a territory in it. A leader with no recovered margin has no capacity to pursue one. Clarity, fluency, and time come first — then the territory becomes obvious and exciting rather than vague and aspirational.

**How to write about it:** Aspirational but grounded. The territory is not described as "unlimited potential" — it is described as a specific, claimable position. For a business owner: the operational AI territory that creates new margin and new revenue. For a creative or influencer: the content and audience intelligence territory they can own and monetise. For a religious leader: the digital ministry territory that reaches further with less friction. For a politician: the AI-informed policy and communication territory that sharpens their mandate. Make it feel real and personal — never theoretical.

## The Central Story Arc

Every section of the landing page should advance this story without interrupting it.

**Act 1 — The moment of honesty.** Most leaders have heard about AI from everyone around them — vendors, their team, the news, their peers. But very few have sat down and answered the question honestly: what does this actually mean for me, in my world, with the people who depend on my leadership? That moment of clarity has not happened yet for most people reading this page.

**Act 2 — The cost of waiting.** The absence of that clarity is already costing something. Time is being lost to work AI could handle. The people who depend on this leader — their team, their congregation, their audience, their voters, their staff — are already asking questions that don't yet have a confident answer. Strategic ground is being left unclaimed. The longer a leader waits, the more all of this compounds.

**Act 3 — The 7 days.** In one week — four live sessions, real tools, structured work — a leader gets four things they didn't walk in with. Day 1 gives them Clarity: what AI actually is and what it means for their world. Day 1 into Day 2 gives them Fluency: the ability to work with AI as a leader, not a user. Day 2 gives them Margin: a real number for how much time they are recovering. Day 3 gives them a Territory: a specific position they declare and can build from.

**Act 4 — The shift their people notice.** This is the underwritten act in most AI programs. But AISC participants don't just leave with personal clarity — they leave as the AI-fluent leader in their world. Their congregation comes to them. Their team defers to them. Their audience trusts them. Their board relies on them. That shift in authority is not a side effect. It is one of the most valuable outcomes of the program, and it starts on Day 1.

**Act 5 — The roadmap bridges it.** The 6-Month AI Stakeholder Roadmap, unlocked on Day 6, turns the Day 3 declaration into 180 days of execution across three phases: proving you can execute, applying your leverage and building the relationships that matter, then extending your authority. Along the way it names the leader's capability gaps, maps who needs to be in their corner, and sets the personal standards they'll hold themselves to. It is the path from the decision to the destination.

**How to talk about the Roadmap's fuller substance (rule, not a one-off):** The full source material is five documents (see AISC_BRIEF.md § "The 6-Month Roadmap System") — the Roadmap itself, the Capability Gap Audit, the AI Stakeholder Identifier, the Personal AI Policy, and the Super Connections relationship guide — branched by the Competitive Position (Optimizer / Producer / Advisor / Educator / Curator) declared on Day 3. **Site copy never names these five documents or frames the Roadmap as "a system" / "not a single plan."** That framing is abstract and tells the reader nothing. Instead, translate each document into the plain value it produces and fold it into a sentence about what the Roadmap *does*: it names your capability gaps (from the Audit), maps who you need in your corner (from the Identifier), and sets your own standards for using AI (from the Personal Policy). The three phases can be named by function (proving execution → applying leverage and relationships → extending authority) without naming "Phase 1/2/3" or day ranges unless the section is already in detail mode (e.g., Demo/Toolbox). Never lead with meta-commentary about the Roadmap's structure ("it's a system, not a plan") — lead with what it does for the leader.

**Verb discipline — Territory vs. Roadmap:** The territory is discovered and declared during the challenge (Day 3, via the Territory Builder). The Roadmap is unlocked on Day 6 and followed, executed, or worked through during the 180 days after the challenge — it is never "built." Building applies to the territory (the leader builds it out, occupies it, makes it real) or to relationships and capability, not to the Roadmap document itself. Do not write "the roadmap you built."

**Act 6 — Coaching makes it inevitable.** For leaders who want to be guided through the roadmap rather than just handed it, the AI Stakeholder Coaching Program coaches them in a live group session once a week. The challenge includes it. There is no additional AISC fee when starting through Coaching.

### The Chief
**Qualifier: Responsibility + Influence + Resources (people and wealth)**

**Who they are — across all industries:**
In the corporate world: CEOs, COOs, CFOs, Founders, Board Members, Commissioners.
In faith communities: Bishops, Senior Pastors, General Overseers, Presiding Officers, Imams with large congregations.
In the public sphere: Elected officials, senior politicians, commissioners, and administrators with budget authority and constituency responsibility.
In entertainment and culture: Celebrities, A-list entertainers, and public figures who have built a business and following around their name.
In the creator economy: Social media influencers and content creators with large, monetizable audiences and a team or agency behind them.
In business: Large business owners with significant staff, operational scale, and financial exposure.

**What they all share:** They are being asked — by their board, their congregation, their voters, their fans, their investors, or their team — what their AI position is. And most of them do not have a clear personal answer yet. Not because they are uninformed, but because nobody has given them a framework built for someone at their level.

**Their internal monologue:**
> "I keep hearing about AI from everyone below me and everyone trying to sell me something. I've approved budgets for it. I've sat through briefings about it. I still don't have a clear position that I'd defend in front of the people I lead. And I'm starting to notice that other people in my space are moving — and I'm not sure toward what."

**The three things AISC delivers for them:**
- **Clarity they can stand behind.** Not a summary of AI — a personal position on what AI means for their domain, their leadership, and their next five years.
- **Time they can reclaim.** The Delegation Matrix shows them, specifically, what they should stop doing — and what AI can handle instead — freeing margin for the decisions only they can make.
- **Territory to build.** A declared AI value territory that turns their clarity into an investment thesis, a governing mandate, or a new revenue stream.

**Copy angles that land for Chiefs:**
- Lead with what they already have: "You have the platform. The resources. The access. What you need is clarity on where AI fits in all of it."
- Speak to their exposure: "The people around you are making AI decisions with or without your direction. AISC makes sure it's with it."
- Frame the territory as theirs to build: "Whether it's a new revenue stream, a governing policy for your organization, or a digital territory in your industry — AISC gives you the framework to declare it and the roadmap to build it."
- **The followers angle — use this, it converts:** Chiefs lead people who are looking to them for a signal on AI. Most of those people are confused and waiting. The chief who gets clear first becomes the authority. Lean into this directly: "Your congregation, your audience, your board, your voters are already asking what AI means. After AISC, you are the one with the answer — and it is yours, not borrowed from a consultant's slide."
- For faith leaders specifically: "Your congregation is asking questions about AI that you should be the one to answer." The territory might be digital ministry, AI-informed pastoral care, or content reach at scale. The fluency angle hits hard here: a pastor who can speak about AI with wisdom and clarity becomes a trusted voice in a space full of noise and fear.
- For politicians: "Your voters, your staff, and your policy agenda are all being shaped by AI. AISC gives you the governance framework to lead that rather than react to it." The fluency angle: the politician who gets clear first sets the agenda. The one who waits responds to someone else's.
- For celebrities and influencers: "Your audience trusts you. When AI confusion reaches them — and it already has — they'll look to you for a signal. AISC makes you that signal." AI fluency is a brand asset for this audience, not just a personal skill.

**What to avoid for Chiefs:**
- "Stay relevant" — implies fragility, never use it
- "Learn AI" — they lead it, they don't study it
- Productivity framing as the main hook — it's the second benefit, not the first
- Any suggestion that they are behind or need to catch up

## What the Copy Must Never Sell

**The territory is not the headline.** It is the destination — and the most powerful destinations are revealed, not announced. Lead with Clarity, then Fluency, then Time, then Territory. Copy that opens with "declare your AI territory" alienates leaders who don't yet have clarity on what AI is. Copy that opens with "finally understand what AI means for you — and become the person your people turn to for answers" speaks to almost everyone.

**The roadmap is not the prize.** The roadmap is the vehicle. Never position it as the main deliverable. It is the answer to "what do I do on Day 8?" — not the reason to join.

**The coaching is not a hard sell.** It is the natural next step for leaders who want to be guided through what they've built. Introduce it as an option that makes the roadmap inevitable, not as a product being closed. Leaders who want coaching will recognize themselves in the description.

**Never apologize for the price, the difficulty, or the exclusivity.** AISC asks for 7 days of real engagement from busy people. That is a high ask. The copy should honor it, not soften it. The filter is a feature. Leaders who understand that this is demanding will trust it more, not less.

**Never use AI jargon as shorthand.** Terms like "LLMs," "prompting," "model," "tokens," or "fine-tuning" do not belong in this copy. Use plain language equivalents: "how to direct AI," "how AI understands your instructions," "the tools inside the platform." If jargon is unavoidable, define it in one plain sentence and move on.

The value of the challenge depends on engagement. The structure is designed to support clarity, but it cannot replace commitment. This challenge does not chase participation.

## The Strategic Logic Behind the Coaching Section

Coaching closes the page because that is where the most committed reader lands. By section 9, they have understood the value (section 2), seen who it is for (section 3), understood the structure (section 4), seen the tools (section 5), read proof (section 6), chosen a tier (section 7), and cleared their objections (section 8). The reader who is still scrolling at this point is not browsing — they are deciding.

Three things make Coaching feel inevitable rather than upsold:

1. **The challenge produces four real wins.** That arc is established across the page. Coaching is not introducing new value — it is asking how the reader wants to continue what has already started.
2. **The Roadmap is where fluency keeps growing.** It is a 180-day filter for decisions, delegation, and AI application. A leader's fluency compounds as they execute it. Most leaders do not want to navigate that development alone — and most won't say it out loud until they feel it's an option.
3. **Coaching is the answer to: "Who walks with me?"** Not a tutor, not a consultant. A guide who coaches the leader once a week, in a group of leaders each executing their own roadmap.

The coaching copy should not compete with the challenge copy or restate it. It should extend it. The reader who recognizes themselves in the Coaching section copy will convert without being closed — because you have written the copy for the version of them who already said yes.
